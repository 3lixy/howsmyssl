Ephemeral Key Support

{{if .EphemeralKeysSupported}}

Cool! Ephemeral keys are supported in the cipher suites your client supports. This dramatically increases your protection against snoopers, including global passive adversaries who scoop up large amounts of encrypted traffic, and store them until their attacks (or their computers) improve.

{{else}}

Shoot. Ephemeral keys are not supported in your client. Without them, global passive adversaries will be able to scoop up all of your encrypted traffic and decode it when their attacks or their computers are faster. This is actually happening.

Session Tickets Support

{{if .SessionTicketsSupported}}

Good! Session tickets are supported in your client. Services you use will be able to scale out their TLS connections much easier with this feature.

{{else}}

Not so good. Session tickets are not supported in your client. Without them, it'll take your connections over TLS longer to work and its harder for services to scale out. Generally, clients with ephemeral key support gets this for free.

{{end}}

{{if .TLSCompressionSupported}}

{{else}}

Good. Your TLS client does not attempt TLS compression, avoiding information leaks from the CRIME attack.

{{end}}

BEAST Attack Vulnerability

{{if .BEASTAttackVuln}}

Ick. Your client is probably open to the BEAST attack. It's using version TLS 1.0 or earlier while also supporting a cipher suite that uses CBC for its block cipher. That combination may leak information. Fixed in TLS 1.1 and later.

{{else}}

Probably okay. Your client is either not vulnerable to the BEAST attack at all, or it supports a newer version of TLS that isn't.

{{end}}
