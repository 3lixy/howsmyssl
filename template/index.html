{{if .SessionTicketsSupported}}

Good! Session tickets are supported in your client. Services you use will be able to scale out their TLS connections much

{{else}}

Not so good. Session tickets are not supported in your client. Without them, it'll take your connections over TLS longer to work and its harder for services to scale out. Generally, clients with ephemeral key support gets this for free.

TLS Compression

{{if .TLSCompressionSupported}}

Uh oh. Your TLS client is using TLS compression. This is really not good. It makes your TLS connections susceptible to the CRIME attack and your encrypted data could be leaked!

{{else}}

Good. Your TLS client does not attempt TLS compression, avoiding information leaks from a CRIME attack.

{{end}}

BEAST Attack Vulnerability

{{if .BEASTAttackVuln}}

Ick. Your client is probably open to the BEAST attack. It's using version TLS 1.0 or earlier while also supporting a cipher suite that uses CBC for its block cipher. That combination may leak information. Fixed in TLS 1.1 and later.

{{else}}

Probably okay. Your client is either not vulnerable to the BEAST attack at all, or it supports a newer version of TLS that isn't.

{{end}}
